Roman Myths

There are many Roman myths that survive today, but among the most famous are:

  • Aeneas, a Trojan hero and son of Venus, fled the burning city of Troy when it fell. He travelled across the Mediterranean to find asylum, eventually landing on the Italian peninsula. His son Iulus founded Alba Longa, a Latin[1] city, and a few centuries later –
  • Romulus and Remus were the result of former king Numitor’s daughter, Rhea Silvia, and Mars, the war god (who was definitely not her husband!) Numitor was overthrown by his brother Amulius, which forced Rhea Silvia to be a Vestal Virgin, but Mars visited her and they resulted in said two children. Paranoid of a future rebellion against his rule, king Amulius ordered a servant to drown Romulus and Remus in the river Tiber, but the servant didn’t want to and just left them there. A she-wolf nurtured them, along with a woodpecker who brought food, until a shepherd Faustulus found the two babies and raised them on their own. Romulus and Remus ended up founding Rome on the seven hills[2], but the two couldn’t decide which hill they wanted to build it on, leading to a dispute where Remus was killed (different versions disagree on who killed Remus). The founding date of the city was declared to be on 21 April 753 BCE.
  • In the early days of Rome, there were many men and not so many women. To fix this, Romulus invited Sabine women to a festival, and the Romans kidnapped the women. The Sabine men were unhappy and were about to fight with the Romans until the women intervened. Peace was made and the Romans and Sabines united.
  • Psyche was even more beautiful than Venus, so Venus asked Cupid to shoot a love arrow at a monster to make Psyche fall in love with it. Cupid accidentally pricked himself with the arrow, and he married Psyche on the condition that she cannot look at his face. Unfortunately, Psyche did, and she did impossible tasks (like Hercules!) to gain back Cupid. She finished and went to the heavens as a goddess.
  • Many other myths (Pyramus and Thisbe, Narcissus and Echo, the Golden Fleece) were taken from Greek culture.
